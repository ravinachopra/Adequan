about canine athritis

canine arthritis affects all kinds of dogs

Canine arthritis is common and can be caused and aggravated by a number of factors, including traumatic injuries, genetic defects, obesity, age. Also, certain breeds with a history of hip and elbow dysplasia are predisposed to it. Canine arthritis acts in some ways a lot like arthritis does in people. Although canine arthritis is often seen as a disease affecting older dogs, it can affect younger dogs as well. In fact, it’s the most common cause of chronic pain in dogs.2 Thankfully, it’s very treatable.

what causes the pain

Canine arthritis is characterized by the degeneration of cartilage, which can cause painful bone-on-bone contact in and around joints. Joints become inflamed and swell, causing dogs to suffer and slow down — that’s when most people notice that their dogs are having an issue.

canine athritis facts:

  • There’s no cure, but it’s very treatable.
  • Young dogs can suffer from it too.
  • Certain breeds are predisposed to it.
Early diagnosis and treatment can make a big difference. If you notice your dog slowing down, talk to your veterinarian right away.

Important safety information

Adequan® Canine (polysultated glycosaminoglycan or PSGAG) should not be used in dogs who are hypersensitive to PSGAG or who have a known or suspected bleeding disorder. It should be used with caution in dogs with renal or hepatic impairment. Side effects in clinical studies (pain at injection site, diarrhea and abnormal bleeding) were mild, transient and self-limiting. In post approval experience, death has been reported in sorne cases; vomiting, anorexia, depression! lethargy and diarrhea have also been reported. The safe use of PSGAG in breeding, pregnant or lactating dogs has not been evaluated. For full product information, view product insert.
